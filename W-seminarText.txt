American identity can be understood as a set of cultural and moral ideals that emphasise freedom, opportunity, self-improvement, and the pursuit of success. According to Patrick N. Allitt, the American Dream is sustained by a “persistent faith in possibility” (Allitt, 2005). This idea is especially relevant when analysing the protagonists of Wall Street and The Wolf of Wall Street, as both films portray men who strongly believe in the possibility of unlimited success and social advancement. However, American identity is not defined only by economic achievement, but also by moral responsibility and socially legitimate success. Therefore, the comparison between the two films allows an examination of how attitudes toward wealth, masculinity, and power reflect changing interpretations of what it means to be “successful” and truly American.
 Allitt, P. N. (2005). The American identity. The Teaching Company.
